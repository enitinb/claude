Friday, May 2

Slept badly. Skipped the walk for the first time since last weekend.

Foggy day. Couldn't seem to start anything. Made a coffee, drank a coffee, made another coffee. Answered emails in a sort of trance.

Around 2pm I called it a half-day mentally and tried to do "low-stakes" tasks. Cleaned up my inbox, archived old folders, found three documents I'd been looking for for weeks. So not unproductive, but not anything that mattered.

Took Mochi to the park at 4 instead. Met a Bernese named Bruno. They were both pleased.

No essay. No reading. Pizza for dinner. Bed 9:45 because I genuinely couldn't keep my eyes open.

Observation I want to record so I don't have to relearn it: the days I don't walk and don't sleep are the days I get nothing done. It's not a mystery. The pattern is two days deep — bad sleep tonight = bad day tomorrow = bad sleep tomorrow night if I don't reset. Reset by 10pm or I lose Saturday too.
