## Deal 1 — Marigold Lane Bakery Group (43 employees, food & bev)
- **Stage at loss:** Final proposal
- **Closed-lost date:** 2026-02-28
- **Won by:** Quillshift
- **Stated reason:** "Quillshift had a Toast (POS) integration and a Gusto (payroll) integration out of the box. We would have needed to build both. Champion told us the integrations sealed it."
- **Our take:** Integration breadth. Their champion liked our product better in the side-by-side, but procurement scored us down on day-one connector coverage.
- **What would have changed it:** A Toast connector and a Gusto connector. Both are on our roadmap (Q3 2026 and Q4 2026 respectively).

## Patterns observed across these losses

1. **Every loss cites integration breadth or depth.** We are not losing on product quality or core agent capability.
2. **Buyers under 50 employees are price-sensitive but value perceived service.** Our self-serve model is a liability with founder-led, risk-averse buyers.
3. **We did not lose a single deal in this window where our integration coverage met the prospect's needs.** That is the lever.
