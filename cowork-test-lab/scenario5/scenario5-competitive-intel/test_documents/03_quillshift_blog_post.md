I want to be transparent with our customers and the industry about where Quillshift is investing in 2026 and why.

The short version: **we're going deeper, not wider.** Our 38-connector library is the broadest in the category, and we'll continue to maintain those connections. But this year, we're not adding many more. Instead, we're going deeper on the workflows that the data tells us actually move the needle for our customers.

Three big bets:

## 1. The "finance workflow stack"

About 60% of usage on our platform — and the highest retention by far — is on finance-adjacent work: invoices, receipts, expense reports, vendor onboarding. In 2026, we're shipping:

- A purpose-built **expense report builder** that ingests receipts from email, drive, or photo and produces a categorized, policy-checked report. Beta opens in June.
- A **vendor onboarding agent** that pulls W-9s, bank info, and contract terms into a structured record.
- Deeper, more opinionated integrations with NetSuite, QuickBooks, and Xero.

## 2. Multi-step agents you can actually trust

This is the part of the product that gets the most pushback in user research. Our agents are good at single steps. Multi-step is where they sometimes go off the rails. We're putting significant effort into a new agent runtime — codename Steward — that makes multi-step plans visible and editable before they run. Expected: H2 2026.

## 3. A better support experience

We hear it in the reviews. Our support response times haven't kept up with our growth. We've hired a new VP of Support, opened our first dedicated APAC support shift in March, and committed to bringing standard-plan response time down to 12 hours by end of Q3.

## What we're *not* doing

- We're not building a desktop app. The web app + native integrations is the right surface area.
- We're not chasing the enterprise segment aggressively this year. SMB-first is paying off.
- We're not racing to the bottom on price.

Thanks for being on the ride.

— Mara
