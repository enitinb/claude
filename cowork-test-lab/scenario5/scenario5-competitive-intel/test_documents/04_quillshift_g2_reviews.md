### ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  "Replaced three tools for our finance team"
*Reviewer: Operations Lead at a 40-person SaaS startup. Posted 2026-04-08.*

We used to manage receipts in one tool, invoices in another, and contracts in a third. Quillshift collapsed that into one workspace and an agent that actually does the categorization work for us. Honestly the best software purchase we've made in two years.

**Pros:** The integration with NetSuite alone justifies the price. Once you set up the workflows, they just work. The voice-memo-to-brief feature is borderline magic.

**Cons:** Pricing is high. We're on the Team plan and at 12 seats it's a noticeable line item. Support is fine but not fast.

### ⭐⭐⭐⭐  "Powerful but support response times are a real issue"
*Reviewer: IT Director at a 110-person services firm. Posted 2026-03-22.*

The product itself is excellent. We had it set up in under a day and it has paid for itself in time savings on AP work alone.

But: support. When something does break — and it has broken twice — getting a response takes a real beat. The last issue took **four days** to get a human reply. For a product that runs against our financial data, that's nerve-wracking.

**Recommend if:** you can tolerate slow support, you have someone internally who can self-troubleshoot.

### ⭐⭐⭐  "Good product, painful pricing for SMBs"
*Reviewer: Founder at a 7-person consultancy. Posted 2026-02-15.*

I want to love this. The product is genuinely smart. But the Team plan jumps from $29 to $79 a seat the moment you need more than one user, which feels designed to punish small teams. And there's a 3-seat minimum on Team, so you're paying for seats you don't have.

We ended up on the Solo plan with creative workarounds. Will probably move to a cheaper competitor when our contract is up.

### ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  "Fastest ROI of any tool we've bought"
*Reviewer: CFO at a 200-person manufacturer. Posted 2026-04-01.*

We bought it for expense automation and have since expanded usage into vendor management. The connector library is incredible — every single tool in our stack already had a native integration.

Support response on the Business plan is fine. (Heard complaints about Team-plan support; we wouldn't accept that SLA so we paid up.)

### ⭐⭐  "Wanted more, got an unfinished agent"
*Reviewer: Operations at a 25-person agency. Posted 2026-01-19.*

The pitch is great. The reality is that the agent does well on single tasks but loses the plot on anything multi-step. We asked it to "go through last quarter's emails and pull all client invoices into a sheet" and it created a sheet with about half the invoices, mis-categorized vendors, and silently skipped four emails it couldn't parse without telling us.

Support did eventually respond and helped us narrow the workflow. But the marketing oversells the multi-step capability.

---

### ⭐⭐⭐⭐  "Great if you live in their integrations"
*Reviewer: Office Manager at a 60-person legal services firm. Posted 2026-04-25.*

If your stack is mainstream (NetSuite, Microsoft 365, HubSpot, Slack), you'll have a fantastic experience. If you use anything niche or homegrown, you'll be doing custom work or living without it.

Pricing is on the high end for the SMB market but the integration coverage is what justifies it for us.
