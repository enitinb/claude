# Quillshift raises $42M Series B as document-AI race intensifies

*Published 2025-12-09 — TheLoop News (fictional)*

Quillshift, the document workflow startup founded in 2022, announced today it has closed a **$42M Series B** led by Greylane Capital, with participation from existing investor Northbeam Ventures.

The round values the company at **$310M post-money**, more than three times its Series A valuation in mid-2024.

CEO Mara Velasquez told TheLoop the funds will be used to "double the engineering team and aggressively expand the SMB sales motion." She positioned Quillshift as "the AI partner for businesses that drown in documents — invoices, contracts, slide decks, the works."

Quillshift's headline product allows customers to drop unstructured files into a workspace and ask an AI agent to organize, extract from, or transform them. The company claims **4,000 paying SMB customers** and an annual revenue run-rate "north of $18M."

## A pivot, quietly

Industry observers noted that the funding announcement is less of a continuation than a re-introduction. Quillshift spent its first two years building PDF redaction tooling targeted at law firms — a product the company has now quietly de-emphasized. The current document-workflow product launched in beta in February 2025.

"They pivoted, and the new motion is working," said one Penumbra Analytics analyst who reviewed the round. "But the market they're now in is the most contested corner of the entire AI software stack."

## Market context

The broader market for AI-native document workflows is estimated at **$5.1B in 2025**, according to a recent CIO-magazine tracking survey, with growth expected to push past $8B in 2026. (Penumbra Analytics' more conservative estimate of $3.2B for 2025 uses a narrower category definition.)

Quillshift competes with a crowded field of SMB-focused players — AscendDocs, Loomtide, and a dozen smaller startups — as well as embedded AI features from incumbent SaaS vendors.

Velasquez declined to comment on pricing strategy beyond confirming the company would "stay premium" and not race to the bottom.
